## Introduction
Influenced by my grandfather who is a physics teacher and my father who know much about circuit, I have developed a keen interest in circuit since primary school. When I was still in **Grade 4 of my primary school**, I took the initiative to attend **the robot interest group**, in which I set out to make robots. In **grade 5 of my primary school**, I became adept at **using electric soldering iron to weld circuits**. My pursuit of more knowledge never stops. During my **junior and senior high school** years, I dedicated myself to learning the circuit related knowledge on the Internet. At this stage, my self-study ability enabled me to **complete PCB drawing, welding, and experiments of multi-stage coilgun**. 

During my undergraduate journey, **I independently executed over 95% of the workload for nine distinctive projects**, which reflect the progression of my academic and research capabilities. By the end of my **freshman year**, I completed **projects 1 to 3**, laying a solid foundation for my subsequent scholarly endeavors. Moving into my **sophomore year**, I delved deeper, successfully carrying out **projects 4 to 6**. In my **junior year**, I completed **projects 7 to 9**, which further showcased my in-depth exploration and academic accomplishments in the realm of research. It is particularly noteworthy that ***projects 5, 8, and 9, with their high level of complexity and depth of inquiry***, stood out as significant milestones in my scholarly work. These projects not only tested my expertise but also strengthened my problem-solving and independent research skills. With my continuous efforts and enduring passion for academic research, ***my academic level has even surpassed that of some students studying in higher grades***.
